NDSS 2023 Research Paper

Drone Security and the Mysterious Case of DJI's DroneID

Paper Abstract

Consumer drones enable high-class aerial video photography, promise to reform the logistics industry, and are already used for humanitarian rescue operations and during armed conflicts. Contrasting their widespread adoption and high popularity, the low entry barrier for air mobility poses many risks to safety, security, and privacy.

Critical Discoveries

  • DroneID protocol lacks encryption
  • Operator privacy compromised
  • Remote privilege escalation possible
  • Safety countermeasures bypassable
  • Mid-flight drone crashes achievable

Research Impact

The paper demonstrates how malicious parties could misuse drones for surveillance, transportation of illegal goods, or cause economic damage by intruding restricted airspace around airports and other critical infrastructure.

16 Vulnerabilities Found

Range from denial of service to arbitrary code execution, with 14 triggerable remotely via smartphone

Methodology

  • Reverse engineering DJI firmware
  • Custom DroneID decoder implementation
  • Novel fuzzing approach
  • Hardware security analysis
